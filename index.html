About Talikala

Talikala was born in response to the challenge of helping prostituted women and empowering them to break free from their "enslavement."


Talikala was founded in August 1987 and designed to be a non-stock, non-profit social development institution comprised of individuals and survivors of prostitution.


Talikala devotes itself to women's empowerment and improving the public's perspective of prostituted women. It views prostitution as a phenomenon that upholds class and gender inequities in our society today. Talikala also pledges to help prostituted women exercise their rights, reclaim their dignity, and define their place in society, as well as build public education channels to alter public perceptions of prostituted women. Talikala's mission is to enhance the quality of life for women who have experienced or survived prostitution and sex trafficking.


Talikala helps women and children affected by prostitution because they see the potential in these individuals and that they, along with us, should defend their rights; thus, we are urged to help them.
